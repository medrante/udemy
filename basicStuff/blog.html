Hello!

My name Trinia Gene L. Medrano. A mouthful isn't it? Filipino names are usually long in nature. My name is actually a combination of my grandparents' names. "Tri" from Trinidad, my paternal grandmother; "nia" from Patrocinia, my maternal grandmother; and "Gene" from Eugenio, my paternal grandfather. My name is my inheritance, a treasure that I will proudly tell everyone about.

Before I tell you more, let me just preface this bio page with this: I would not have gotten this far without God and the support of my friends and family. Everything I do is impacted by them. Now, let's go on.

I will enter my third year of Computer Engineering this fall. My love for technology grew when I realized how privileged I am to be able to access such things and how much I can have an impact in the world. Many third world countries do not have the resources to implement such technology. June 13 2018

This is the second part of the blog

This past year has been a whirlwind, to say the least. After finishing my self-guided tour around South Korea, I visited my hometown in the Philippines for two months, where I ate fresh food, jumped off a tiny cliff, and breathed in the fresh air in the provinces. For a week, I along with other cadets from AFROTC got to be live patients in the Air Mobility Guardian Exercise in Washington. After that, I was off to Erie, Pennsylvania for another adventure. I thought this would be a fun one, but that was not the case. At least now I know that I will not be accepting co-ops in small cities no matter how fun and interesting the work is. I am thankful and blessed for completing this stage of my life even with all the travelling, working in my first co-op, balancing Air Force ROTC commitments, juggling a heavy school load, and going through Field Training. Check out my Year Two in Review for more.

Aside from Air Force ROTC and school, I regularly volunteer at Crossroads Church, climb at the UC climbing wall, and do road trips with my family. I also love donuts and coffee. I love travelling, and I was able to go on my first solo trip abroad in the summer of 2017. Visiting South Korea was part of an Honors Program experience, but I took the chance to fly to the Philippines for a couple months before I came back to my busy academic life. If you want to know what I did in South Korea, check out my Experiences. If you want to know what I did in the Philippines (send me an email).

TLDR; Faith, family, friends, passion, and hard work are where it's at. You may have all these dreams in mind; but without those five things, you probably won't get that far.
